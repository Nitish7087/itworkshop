Taste of Jaipur: Local Delicacies

Rajasthani cuisine is known for its rich flavors, unique cooking techniques, and vibrant colors

Dal Baati Churma

A traditional Rajasthani meal consisting of baked wheat balls (baati), lentil curry (dal), and sweetened crushed wheat (churma).

Where to find it: Chokhi Dhani, Spice Court, Lakshmi Misthan Bhandar

Pyaaz Kachori

Deep-fried pastry filled with a spicy onion filling, served with tangy tamarind and mint chutneys. A favorite breakfast and snack item.

Where to find it: Rawat Mishtan Bhandar, Samrat Restaurant, Laxmi Misthan Bhandar

Ghewar

A disc-shaped sweet cake made from flour soaked in ghee and milk with a crispy honeycomb texture, topped with nuts and saffron.

Where to find it: Laxmi Misthan Bhandar, Rawat Misthan Bhandar, Sodhani Sweets

Laal Maas

A fiery meat curry made with red Mathania chilies, yogurt, and warm spices. It's a traditional Rajput hunting dish.

Where to find it: Handi Restaurant, Spice Court, 1135 AD at Amber Fort

Taste of Kerala: Local Delicacies

Kerala cuisine is renowned for its aromatic spices, coconut, and seafood dishes

Appam with Stew

Lacy, bowl-shaped rice pancakes served with a mild and creamy coconut stew filled with vegetables or meat.

Where to find it: Paragon Restaurant (Kochi), Hotel Saravana Bhavan, Grand Hotel

Kerala Fish Curry

Spicy and tangy curry made with kokum or tamarind, coconut milk, and fresh fish. Usually served with rice or tapioca.

Where to find it: Karimeen Restaurant (Alleppey), Fish Curry Restaurant (Fort Kochi), Seagull Restaurant

Puttu and Kadala Curry

Steamed rice flour cake layered with coconut, served with spicy black chickpea curry. A traditional breakfast dish.

Where to find it: Indian Coffee House, Kayees Rahmathulla Hotel (Kochi), Aryas Hotel

Ela Sadya

A traditional Kerala feast served on banana leaf with rice, various vegetable preparations, pickles, payasam and more.

Where to find it: Mother's Veg Plaza (Kochi), Saravana Bhavan, Any traditional Kerala restaurant

Taste of Goa: Local Delicacies

Goan cuisine blends Portuguese influences with coastal Indian flavors, known for its seafood, coconut, and spices.

Goan Fish Curry

A tangy and spicy curry made with coconut, tamarind, and local spices, usually prepared with kingfish or pomfret.

Where to find it: Ritz Classic (Panaji), Mum's Kitchen, Vinayak Family Restaurant

Prawn Balchão

A spicy Goan prawn pickle made with vinegar and red chilies, often served with steamed rice or bread.

Where to find it: Souza Lobo, Chef Fernando’s Nostalgia, Ourem 88

Pork Vindaloo

A hot and sour pork curry marinated in vinegar, garlic, and spices, influenced by Portuguese cuisine.

Where to find it: Hospedaria Venite, The Fisherman’s Wharf, Viva Panjim

Bebinca

A traditional Goan layered dessert made with coconut milk, egg yolks, sugar, and ghee. Best served warm with ice cream.

Where to find it: Confeitaria 31 de Janeiro, Mr. Baker 1922, Nostalgia Restaurant
